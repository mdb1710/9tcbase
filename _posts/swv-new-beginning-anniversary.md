## The Rise of SWV and the Anticipation for "New Beginning"

In the golden era of 90s R&B, few groups captured the hearts of listeners like SWV (Sisters With Voices). After their blockbuster debut, "It's About Time," featuring timeless hits like "Weak" and "Right Here (Human Nature Remix)," anticipation built for their follow-up. Fans and critics alike wondered if SWV could recreate the magic.

When "New Beginning" dropped, it didn't just meet expectations—it exceeded them. The album displayed SWV’s growth, both as vocalists and storytellers, and cemented their status as R&B royalty.

---

## A New Sound and Maturing Voices

"New Beginning" saw SWV collaborating with renowned producers like The Neptunes (in one of their earliest major placements), as well as Brian Alexander Morgan, who helped define their original sound. The result? A fusion of sultry harmonies, hip hop-influenced beats, and thoughtful lyricism.

Fans noticed a subtle shift with "New Beginning." The lyrics reflected maturity, tackling themes of self-discovery, heartbreak, and resilience. Musically, the trio—Cheryl “Coko” Gamble, Tamara “Taj” Johnson, and Leanne “Lelee” Lyons—experimented with new sounds, including a heavier use of live instrumentation.

---

## Impact and Reception

Upon release, "New Beginning" climbed to #9 on the Billboard 200 and was quickly certified platinum. The lead single, "You're the One," became a summer anthem, showcasing SWV's ability to blend R&B, hip hop, and new jack swing elements.

Critics praised the trio for evolving without abandoning what made them special—stunning harmonies and emotional delivery. Over time, the album became a touchstone for fans and a source of inspiration for future R&B artists.

## The Top 10 Best Songs from "New Beginning"

To honor the 29th anniversary, here's a curated list of the top 10 track's from "New Beginning." These selections reflect both fan favorites and critical acclaim.

1. ### **You're the One**
   The breakout single, bursting with attitude and flirtatious energy. Its infectious hook and uptempo beat made it an instant radio staple.

2. ### **Use Your Heart**
   Produced by The Neptunes, this sultry ballad is often hailed as a hidden gem. The smooth production and heartfelt lyrics make it a standout love song.

3. ### **It's All About U**
   An evocative mid-tempo track about devotion. SWV’s harmonies shine, and the groove is impossible to resist.

4. ### **Fine Time**
   Driven by a bouncy bassline and percussive rhythm, this song addresses the struggles of making time for love in busy lives.

5. ### **Whatcha Need**
   A fan favorite for its playful melody and catchy chorus, spotlighting the group’s charisma.

6. ### **When This Feeling**
   This ballad showcases Coko’s powerhouse vocals, as she expresses the complexity of holding onto love. This was also produced by the Neptunes.

7. ### **Come and Get Some**
   Featuring E-40, this collaboration pushes SWV into harder-edged hip hop territory without losing their signature lushness.

8. ### **New Beginning (Interlude)**
   More than just a bridge, this ethereal interlude underscores the album's theme of growth and transformation.

9. ### **Love Is So Amazin'**
   A smooth, romantic jam that harkens back to the classic R&B sound while updating it for the late 90s.

10. ### **Where Is the Love**
    Closing out the top ten, this song’s searching vocals and yearning lyrics close the album on a heartfelt note.

*Honorable Mention: “On & On,” “Dear Mom,” and “Hold On”—each deserving of their own listen!*

## SWV’s Influence: Then and Now

Nearly three decades later, the impact of "New Beginning" is still felt. SWV helped broaden the soundscape of R&B, paving the way for female trios like Destiny’s Child and 702. Many contemporary artists cite SWV as an influence, crediting their sophisticated vocal arrangements and honest songwriting.

"Use Your Heart" is particularly notable for being one of The Neptunes’ earliest major production credits. Years later, Pharrell Williams and Chad Hugo would dominate the 2000s, but their signature sound traces back to this track—a testament to SWV’s knack for ear for talented collaborators.

## Looking Back: 29 Years of Timeless Music

With nostalgia for 90s R&B at an all-time high, "New Beginning" feels as relevant as ever. Its messages of growth, love, and optimism are universal. The album reminds us that even when we encounter heartbreak, there’s always a new beginning on the horizon.

For fans old and new, it’s the perfect time to revisit this record—whether you’re playing it on your original CD, vinyl, or streaming it for the first time. Let SWV’s voices remind you why their music continues to stand the test of time.

## Final Thoughts

The 29th anniversary of "New Beginning" is more than a celebration of great music; it’s a reminder of the power of reinvention. SWV entered the mid-90s with fresh energy, and the result was a classic album that resonates today.

So, let’s cue up "You’re the One,” turn up the volume, and raise a glass to 29 years of SWV’s enduring magic. Here’s to many more anniversaries—and new beginnings—to come.
