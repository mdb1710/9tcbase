Roberta Flack's voice. It's an instrument of unparalleled beauty, capable of conveying a spectrum of emotions from the deepest sorrow to the most radiant joy.  More than just a singer, she's a storyteller, weaving narratives through her music that resonate across generations.  Her artistry transcends genre, seamlessly blending soul, jazz, pop, and folk into a unique and unforgettable sound.  Choosing just 20 of her best songs is a challenge, but this list attempts to capture the breadth and depth of her incredible career.

## The Essentials:

"Killing Me Softly with His Song":  This is, without a doubt, the Roberta Flack signature song.  Its haunting melody and poignant lyrics, telling the story of someone deeply moved by a performer's song, struck a chord with millions.  It's a timeless classic, instantly recognizable and eternally beautiful.

"The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face":  Originally a folk song, Flack's rendition transformed it into a soul-stirring ballad.  Her delivery is breathtaking, filled with raw emotion and vulnerability.  It's a song that perfectly captures the overwhelming feeling of new love.

"Feel Like Makin' Love": This smooth, sensual track showcases Flack's versatility.  It's a departure from some of her more melancholic work, but it demonstrates her ability to deliver a powerful and seductive performance.

"Where Is the Love" (with Donny Hathaway):  The collaboration with Donny Hathaway was pure magic.  Their voices blended seamlessly, creating a musical synergy that's rarely been matched.  "Where Is the Love" is a soulful plea for understanding and connection, a timeless message that remains relevant today.

"You've Got a Friend":  While Carole King wrote the song, Flack's interpretation is truly special.  Her soulful delivery adds a layer of depth and emotion that makes it uniquely her own.

## Deeper Cuts and Hidden Gems:

"Tonight, I Celebrate My Love" (with Peabo Bryson):  Another powerful duet, this time with Peabo Bryson.  Their voices soar together, creating a romantic anthem that's perfect for any celebration of love.

"Back Together Again" (with Donny Hathaway):  This upbeat and joyful track showcases the playful side of Flack and Hathaway's musical partnership.  It's a feel-good song that's guaranteed to lift your spirits.

"Jesse":  A poignant and heartbreaking ballad about the loss of a loved one. Flack's voice conveys the depth of grief with incredible sensitivity.

"Reverend Lee": This song tells a story with vivid imagery and powerful vocals. It showcases Flack's ability to inhabit a character and bring their story to life.

"Sunday and Sister Jones": A soulful and spiritual song that speaks to the power of faith and community.  Flack's performance is both moving and inspiring.

## Exploring Her Range:

"Bridge Over Troubled Water":  Simon & Garfunkel's classic gets the Roberta Flack treatment, and the result is stunning.  Her soulful interpretation adds a new dimension to the song's message of hope and support.

"Will You Still Love Me Tomorrow":  This Carole King-penned classic is given a soulful makeover by Flack.  Her rendition is both tender and powerful, capturing the uncertainty and vulnerability of love.

"Just Like a Woman":  Bob Dylan's song is reinterpreted by Flack with a soulful and feminine perspective.  Her version is both insightful and moving.

"Go Up Moses":  A powerful spiritual that showcases Flack's vocal range and her deep connection to her roots.

"Ode to Billie Joe":  This narrative ballad, originally by Bobbie Gentry, is given a dramatic and soulful interpretation by Flack.

Later Classics and Collaborations:

"Making Love":  A smooth and romantic ballad that showcases Flack's vocal prowess.  It's a perfect example of her ability to convey emotion through song.

"25 or 6 to 4":  A surprising but successful cover of the Chicago hit.  Flack's soulful rendition adds a new layer of meaning to the song.

"More Than You Know":  A classic love song given a contemporary feel by Flack.  Her performance is both elegant and heartfelt.

"I Say a Little Prayer":  This Burt Bacharach classic is given a soulful and uplifting treatment by Flack.

"This Is It": A powerful and inspiring song that speaks to the resilience of the human spirit.  It's a perfect closing track to this list, showcasing Flack's enduring talent and her ability to connect with listeners on a deep level.

This list is just a starting point for exploring the incredible musical legacy of Roberta Flack.  Her artistry is timeless, and her music continues to inspire and move listeners around the world.  Whether you're a longtime fan or just discovering her music for the first time, you're sure to be captivated by her voice and her incredible talent.
